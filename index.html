What is safeguarding?

Safeguarding is the practice of protecting people's health, well-being, and human rights, and ensuring they are free from abuse, harm, and neglect. It's a collective responsibility for everyone, especially when it comes to children, young people, and vulnerable adults.

What are the signs of abbuse and neglect?

Here are just a few sign of abuse or neglet:

  • The person may have injuries or re-occoring injuries that they cant explain how they got them. Keep an eye out especially for injuries in non-accidental areas of the body.
  • The person may look malnurished or have sudden weight loss or weight gain. As well as having poor hygine.
  • The person may have a lack of self-confidence and feel that they are to blame. They may appear withdrawn, tearful or angy/aggressive.
  • They may have limited access to money, or have a sudden change in their finances. You might notice unusual financial documents, or documents related to their finances going missing.
  • They may be isolated from friends and family.
What actions can I take?

If you have a safeguarding concern, you can take the following actions:

  • Assess the urgency: Determine if the situation is an immediate danger
  • Contact the appropriate authorities: Depending on the situation, you can contact the police, social services, or other relevant authorities
  • Gather information: Collect relevant details, including who, what, why, when, and where
  • Express your concerns: Clearly and concisely communicate your concerns to the relevant authority
  • Maintain confidentiality: Protect the confidentiality of the situation
  • Document your actions: Keep precise records of your actions
